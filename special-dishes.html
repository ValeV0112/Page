Problematic

01.

Overuse of
School Supplies

Schools often rely heavily on traditional materials like paper, pencils, and erasers, resulting in excessive waste and resource consumption. This overuse not only impacts natural resources but also contributes to significant waste in educational institutions. BioBloom tackles this issue by introducing recycled and reusable alternatives, encouraging students and teachers to adopt sustainable practices. By promoting a culture of conscious consumption, we aim to reduce the overall use of materials in schools.

02.

Environmental
Pollution

The manufacturing and disposal processes of conventional school supplies lead to pollution, affecting ecosystems and raising our carbon footprint. Plastics, synthetic chemicals, and non-biodegradable waste are common pollutants from traditional school supplies, posing long-term environmental threats. BioBloom addresses this by offering eco-friendly materials, like seed-embedded pencils and biodegradable paper, which minimize environmental impact. By using these green alternatives, BioBloom encourages schools to lessen their ecological footprint and contribute to a healthier planet.

BioBloom is a sustainability project aimed at reducing paper waste in educational settings by promoting the use of eco-friendly materials. It focuses on recycling and reusing paper while introducing seed pencils that can grow into plants after use. The initiative is designed for students at the Colegio Bilingüe Nuestra Señora del Rosario in Bogotá, fostering environmental awareness and encouraging sustainable practices among upper-grade students.
